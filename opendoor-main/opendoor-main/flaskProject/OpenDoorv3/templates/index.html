The customer is your target market for whom you should brainstorm possible problems and solutions This step requires you to identify as many realistic problems as you can for the customer you have been assigned

We hope you enjoyed your ideation process. The ideation process is the first step to developing a business idea. You are not expected to have the perfect idea at this stage, so do not criticize yourself, or praise yourself. The process is only beginning. We have created a series of questions that help you with planning your business idea and taking the next step to help develop your idea into reality.

What is your company going to make?

(We recommend you keep this answer as concise as possible)

Is the problem you have identified a recurring problem for your customer?

Why did you choose this idea to solve the problem? Are you aware of any existing solutions working towards the same idea?

What is new and unique about your business idea? Can you think of an alternative solution that your customers are already using to address their problem?

Who are your competitors, who will be your competitors and what challenges are you most fearful of?

How will you make money?

(What is your business model)

How will you attract users? If your business idea is dependent on more than one factor (s), how will you overcome that?

How will you test your idea? How will you try to understand that your idea can attain a product-market-fit?

To what extent will you pursuit your business idea if it is able to be invested by venture capital?
